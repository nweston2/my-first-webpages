Web Developer Report

As part of the course that helped me create this site, we were put in groups and simulated client/developer relationships. Each student was a client for their own website, a lead developer for someone else's site, and a junior developer for two additional sites.

Client

I recently started creating bonsai as a hobby, so it was the logical content for my site. There is a plethora of images of bonsai online, so it was not difficult to find images to use.

I communicated with my developer through email. We didn't have any major issues with communication, though I was not entirely satisfied with the results. Large parts of the content I provided to put in the website were not used and the colors I gave were used seemingly at random. Next time I hire a team to do a project for me, I will need to find a better way to communicate my needs. There seems to be a delicate balance between giving too much creative licence and just making the site yourself before handing it over for nitpicks.

Lead Developer

Email was the primary method of communication for this website. This is how I talked to my junior developers when I needed. I also used email to talk to the client.

Github worked well for us. I shared the link to the pages site with the client so she could monitor our progress and make any suggestions as we worked. Our work came together seamlessly using github.

I was rather pleased with the outcome of the website. I had a hard time contacting one of my junior developers. The content all made it into the site, but their page is not formatted well. Aside from that, everything turned out well.

Junior Developer

The first website on which I was a junior developer was an informational site about Star Wars. The main page is a chronological timeline of Star Wars and the child sites are about the clients favorite movies. I was in charge of the page about A New Hope.

The main issue I ran into was confusion about the site wireframe. The wireframe was very clear about what the client wanted, but my lead decided not to follow it in the creation of the site. This made creating my site difficult because I had to create my page independent of the rest of the site in order to follow the client's instructions. Other than that, things went off without a hitch. We had no problems with communication or github that I am aware of. We used email as our primary form of communication. I think overall the website turned out pretty well.

The second site I was supposed to assist in developing had some issues with the client. We never recieved a site plan with any real information on what we were supposed to make. As you can probably imagine, this made it difficult to make.

General Experience and Reflections

Overall I think the most difficult aspect of website creation is communication. Things go smoothly when everyone can converse and express their needs and confusions. When someone doesn't respond, however, things quickly become vastly more difficult. If I were to do the project again, I would probably get together with my teams and decide on a form of communication that works for everyone that we are all likely to be able to respond to.

I really liked this project. It was fun to work on a bunch of different websites, especially since some included content I would never think of making myself. I definitely feel more confident in my ability to work on real world projects after this experience. I was able to do good work, even with some complications. Given my new experiences, I even know some of the pitfalls of projects like this and can work to overcome similar future issues.
